The Story Behind DollhouseMCP

It started with a simple problem: After two years of building with AI tools, I found cool and useful prompts scattered across Reddit, Discord, and LinkedIn. But it became messy and hard to find what I wanted when I needed it. I had an incredibly bad system for organizing them.

So I started building a simple catalog system using Claude Code and MCP. Day one: proof of concept worked. Week two: hey, this is pretty cool. Month two: I realized I could create a collection for everyone to use and share.

The breakthrough: Instead of hoping AI understands what you want every time (vibe coding), you build individual tools using natural language that persist and evolve (vibe tooling). These tools remember your preferences, maintain context, and improve over time.

For those who remember Tomorrowish, this builds on more than a decade of ML/AI work. The patents in natural language processing we received provided me a unique lens for blending programmatic tools with LLMs.

Today: DollhouseMCP is incredibly useful every day. I'm using it right now for copy editing through verbal instructions. It's helped me solve dozens of problems. And it's remarkably fun to use. The audio skill took five minutes to build, which felt incredibly sci-fi.
